About project

In recent years, we have seen the enormous advance of technology in artificial intelligence and image analysis, which has revolutionized multiple fields, from creating surreal images with very specific details to recognizing a person. However, this use of AI and intelligent images has not reached the area of ​​biology and agriculture with the required force, since there are many challenges that limit this widespread adoption. Some problems include the low quantity and quality of data, interdisciplinary experience, where many professionals do not have advanced training in AI, and the biological complexity of organisms.

Objects

Wildlife monitoring is carried out for various reasons, such as biodiversity conservation and management, population health assessment, pest control, behavioral patterns, species or population activity, detection of environmental impacts, and more. The traditional wildlife monitoring process in general is based on observation and in many cases the collection of some organisms for identification. This monitoring involves manual identification and can be slow in many cases since it involves too much valuable time from the researcher who has to be an expert in their taxonomy, so it depends a lot on experience. In addition, cash monitoring requires information from field data such as environmental variables (temperature, location, wind direction, precipitator, among others).
